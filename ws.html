🌊 Sources of Water

Classification, characteristics, and suitability for water supply

🏔️
Atmospheric Water
Rain water, snow melt. Purest natural form — low TDS, soft, may dissolve CO₂ → acidic. Collected via rooftop harvesting. Not reliable for large supplies.
🌊
Surface Water
Rivers, lakes, reservoirs, ponds. High turbidity, subject to contamination, seasonal variation. Requires full treatment but available in large quantity.
🌍
Ground Water
Wells (open/tube), springs, artesian wells. Low turbidity, bacteriologically safer, high mineral content, temperature stable, requires less treatment.
🏞️
Types of Wells
TypeDepthLiningFeature

🏗️ Types of Intakes ▼
River Intake: Located on river banks or mid-river. Includes screens to prevent entry of debris. Types: direct intake, infiltration intake, submerged intake.

Reservoir/Lake Intake: Tower intake or submerged intake. Tower intakes have multiple openings at different depths to draw water from cleanest level.

Spring Intake: Spring box or spring chamber is constructed. Flow is gravity-based. Suitable for hilly areas.

Key Design Consideration: Intake should be located upstream of any source of pollution, away from waste discharge points, and where silt content is minimum.
🌿 Watershed Management ▼
The catchment area from which runoff is collected. Key terms:

Yield of Catchment: Volume of water available after losses (ET, infiltration).
Safe Yield: Dependable yield during critical dry years.
Runoff Coefficient: Ratio of runoff to rainfall. Varies 0.1 (forests) to 0.9 (impervious surfaces).
Rational Formula: Q = C·i·A (m³/s), C = runoff coefficient, i = rainfall intensity, A = area in hectares.

Bacteriological Standards
Indicator Organism: E. coli (Escherichia coli) — indicates fecal contamination.

MPN (Most Probable Number): Statistical estimate of coliform count per 100 mL.

BIS Standard:
• Coliform: 0 per 100 mL in treated piped supply
• E. coli: Absent
• Plate count: ≤ 100 CFU/mL at 37°C

Tests: Presumptive test, Confirmative test, Completed test (Three-step series)
🌱
BOD & DO
DO (Dissolved Oxygen):
Acceptable for drinking: >4 mg/L (not a BIS limit for drinking but important for quality)
Saturation at 25°C: ~8.24 mg/L

BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand):
5-day BOD at 20°C = BOD₅
For potable water: BOD should be very low (<2 mg/L ideal)

COD > BOD (COD includes non-biodegradable oxidisable matter)
COD/BOD ratio: >2 indicates non-biodegradable waste dominance.

Plain Sedimentation
Removal of settleable solids by gravity without chemicals.

Settling Velocity (Stoke's Law):
Applicable for laminar flow conditions (Re < 1)
Stoke's Law
Vs = [g(ρs − ρw) × d²] / (18μ)
Vs = settling velocity, d = particle diameter, μ = dynamic viscosity
Surface Overflow Rate (SOR)
SOR = Q / A = Vs (at overflow)
Q = flow rate, A = surface area of tank; Typical: 12–25 m³/m²/day

📈
Breakpoint Chlorination
As Cl₂ dose increases:
1. Reacts with reducing substances (oxidation)
2. Forms chloramines (combined residual increases)
3. Chloramines destroyed (residual drops to zero) ← BREAKPOINT
4. Beyond breakpoint: free chlorine residual increases

Free residual chlorine > combined residual chlorine in germicidal efficiency.
⏰
Contact Time (CT)
CT = Concentration × Time

Chlorine contact time: Minimum 30 minutes
Ozone: 4–10 minutes (strongest oxidant)
UV: No residual; not suitable for distribution

Factors affecting efficiency:
Lower pH → more effective
Higher temperature → more effective
Lower turbidity → more effective
🔥 CHLORINE DEMAND
Chlorine Demand = Chlorine Applied − Residual Chlorine
Applied Dose = Chlorine Demand + Required Residual (0.2 mg/L minimum)
🧂
Lime-Soda Softening
Chemicals used: Ca(OH)₂ (Lime) + Na₂CO₃ (Soda ash)

Reactions:
Temporary Ca hardness: Ca(HCO₃)₂ + Ca(OH)₂ → 2CaCO₃↓ + 2H₂O
Permanent Ca hardness: CaSO₄ + Na₂CO₃ → CaCO₃↓ + Na₂SO₄
Temporary Mg hardness: MgCO₃ + Ca(OH)₂ → Mg(OH)₂↓ + CaCO₃↓
Permanent Mg hardness: MgSO₄ + Ca(OH)₂ → Mg(OH)₂↓ + CaSO₄ (+ soda)

Residual hardness: ~80 mg/L (CaCO₃) — excess lime recarbonation needed
🔄
Zeolite / Ion Exchange
Zeolite process: Na₂Z (zeolite) exchanges Na⁺ for Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺
Ca²⁺ + Na₂Z → CaZ + 2Na⁺

Regeneration: CaZ + 2NaCl → Na₂Z + CaCl₂

Removes: Both temporary and permanent hardness
Cannot remove: Iron, turbidity (must pre-treat)
Residual hardness: Nearly zero (complete softening)
Ion exchange resins (modern) work similarly, more efficient.

🏗️ Intake & Conveyance

Intake structures, pipe flow equations, open channel hydraulics

📐
Pipe Flow — Key Equations
Hazen-Williams (most used)
V = 0.8492 · C · R⁰·⁶³ · S⁰·⁵⁴
C = roughness coefficient, R = hydraulic radius, S = slope; Q = AV
Darcy-Weisbach
hf = f · L · V² / (2 · g · D)
f = friction factor, L = length, D = diameter, V = velocity
Manning's Equation
V = (1/n) · R^(2/3) · S^(1/2)
n = Manning's roughness, R = hydraulic radius, S = hydraulic slope

🔗 Pipes in Series and Parallel ▼
Series: Same flow (Q), total head loss = Σhf
hf_total = hf₁ + hf₂ + hf₃ + ...

Parallel: Same head loss, total Q = ΣQ
hf₁ = hf₂ = hf₃ = ... (head loss equal)
Q_total = Q₁ + Q₂ + Q₃ + ...

Equivalent Pipe: One pipe that replaces the system with same Q and hf. For series: L_eq/D_eq⁵ = L₁/D₁⁵ + L₂/D₂⁵ (using Hazen-Williams, C same)
🔄 Hardy-Cross Method — Step by Step ▼
Used for analysis of pipe networks (loops).

Steps:
1. Assume initial flows (Q₀) satisfying continuity at each node.
2. Calculate head loss: hf = k·Q^n (n=1.85 for H-W, n=2 for D-W)
3. Calculate ΔQ = −ΣhF / (n·Σhf/Q) for each loop
4. Correct flows: Q_new = Q_old + ΔQ
5. Repeat until ΔQ < acceptable tolerance (usually <1–2 L/s)

Sign Convention: Clockwise flows = positive, anti-clockwise = negative. Σhf for loop should = 0 at convergence.

For Hazen-Williams: hf = k·Q^1.85; k = 10.67·L / (C^1.85 · D^4.87)

🔧 Distribution Systems

Layout patterns, pressure requirements, pipes, valves, and appurtenances

📐
Dead End / Tree System
Single pipe runs with branches. Simple design, no loops. Problem: if main breaks, all downstream areas suffer. Low capital cost. Suitable for small towns with irregular street layout.
Simple towns
🔄
Grid Iron System
All pipes interconnected in grid. Water can reach any point from multiple directions. Best reliability and fire fighting. Most expensive. Requires Hardy-Cross analysis.
Best system
⭕
Ring / Circular System
Pipes form a loop or ring around the town. Good reliability. If one section breaks, flow reverses. Intermediate cost. Suitable for towns with symmetrical layout.
Medium towns

Valves in Distribution
  • Sluice valve (gate valve): Full on/off; least resistance when open
  • Air valve: Releases trapped air at high points
  • Reflux / Check valve: Prevents backflow (non-return valve)

⚡ Pumps & Storage

Types of pumps, pump selection, storage tanks, elevated reservoirs

⚡
Centrifugal Pumps
Most common in water supply. Impeller rotates → kinetic energy → pressure energy. Types: radial flow, axial flow, mixed flow.
Power & Efficiency
Power input = γ·Q·H / η (watts)
η = Water power / Shaft power
γ = 9810 N/m³, Q = m³/s, H = head in m, η = efficiency
Specific Speed
Ns = N·√Q / H^(3/4)
Ns < 50: radial; 50–150: mixed; >150: axial flow pump
🏛️
Storage Tanks
TypeStorage Capacity
Clear water sump2–4 hours demand
Elevated service reservoir1/3rd of daily demand
Ground level reservoir1 day's demand
Height of ESR: Determined by residual pressure at remote point + friction losses + elevation difference. Typically 15–20 m above service area.
🔬 Pump Affinity Laws ▼
For a centrifugal pump with speed change (N₁ → N₂):

Q₂/Q₁ = N₂/N₁ (Flow varies directly with speed)
H₂/H₁ = (N₂/N₁)² (Head varies as square of speed)
P₂/P₁ = (N₂/N₁)³ (Power varies as cube of speed)

NPSH (Net Positive Suction Head): Must be provided to prevent cavitation. Available NPSH must exceed required NPSH. If cavitation occurs → noise, vibration, impeller damage.
💧 Water Hammer ▼
Pressure surge caused by sudden stoppage/change of flow velocity (e.g., sudden valve closure).

Joukowski's equation: ΔP = ρ·c·ΔV
c = wave speed = √(K/ρ) for rigid pipe; adjusted for elastic pipes.

Prevention: Slow valve closure, pressure relief valves, surge tanks, air vessels, flywheel on pumps, non-return valves.
